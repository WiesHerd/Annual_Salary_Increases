# What If You Could Build Your Own Merit Increase Process — Without the Enterprise Price Tag?

If you've ever managed physician compensation, you know the drill. Annual merit increases sound straightforward until you're buried in spreadsheets, juggling market survey data from multiple vendors, trying to apply experience bands, FMV guardrails, and a merit matrix — all while staying within budget.

These processes are complicated. They require pulling together provider rosters, market benchmarks, evaluation scores, and supplemental payment data from completely different systems. Most organizations either cobble it together in Excel or pay six figures for an enterprise platform that takes months to implement.

I decided to build a third option.

## Meritly: A Merit Increase Modeler You Can Make Your Own

I've been building an application called Meritly that lets compensation teams upload their own data streams — provider rosters, market surveys (MGMA, Sullivan Cotter, or your own), evaluations, and payment history — and immediately start working with them. No months-long implementation. No IT tickets. Just drag, drop, map your columns, and go.

The real power is in the policy engine. Instead of hiding your increase logic inside a spreadsheet formula that nobody can audit, you define actual policies: guardrails that cap increases when TCC exceeds a percentile threshold, merit matrices tied to performance scores, modifiers for high-wRVU producers, experience-band targeting. Each policy has clear conditions, a priority order, and a full trace so you can see exactly why a provider received a specific recommendation.

The salary review workspace brings it all together — budget tracking, market percentile comparisons, equity recommendations, and inline editing so you can approve, adjust, and finalize increases in one place. You can compare scenarios side by side to see how different policy configurations affect your budget and your providers.
